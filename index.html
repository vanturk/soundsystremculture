Sounds System Culture is Where Harlem’s Voice Becomes the World’s Rhythm

Built by Harlem, shared with the world.

Sound System Culture is rooted in Harlem’s history, powered by its people, and open to the world.

Our mission is redefining what radio and media can be — a living, breathing art form that empowers community voices and transforms storytelling into sound. We’re building a strong, independent station that bridges culture, creativity, and connection, giving Harlem — and the world — a new way to listen, share, and belong.

We are calling it Art Radio, the beating heart of Harlem’s creative renaissance — where artists, storytellers, thinkers, and everyday residents create content, build community, and inspire a global audience through sound, rhythm, and narrative.

ArtRadio isn’t just about music — it’s about empowerment. It’s about giving people a stage to express their truth, create together, and reshape how radio interacts with art, culture, and commerce.

What We Aim to Accomplish.

  • Independent Station & Shows

    Create a signature sound with original shows and hosts that reflect Harlem’s diversity and artistic spirit. This builds credibility, identity, and audience loyalty.

  • Community as Creator

    Invite residents, students, and artists to produce and submit stories, sets, and ideas. This turns listeners into collaborators and fosters community ownership.

  • Media Innovation

    Develop our own 'Art Radio' sound design, transitions, and digital broadcasting tools. This differentiates and protects the brand.

  • Partnership in Purpose

    Redefine advertising as collaboration — sponsors help tell the story. This creates meaningful brand partnerships.

  • Harlem Music Fest

    A festival born from the station — showcasing talent, community art, and the culture we cultivate. It connects the digital platform to real-world experiences.

  • Leadership & Mentorship

    Craig and key mentors guide, empower, and support emerging voices. This builds a legacy of trust, creativity, and opportunity.

About us.

  • Martha Cinader

    Executive Director

    Martha Cinader is a poet, author, and multimedia producer whose creative journey began in the vibrant spoken word and jazz scenes of 1980s and ’90s NYC. She has performed at renowned venues and festivals including the Edinburgh Festival Fringe, the Panasonic Village Jazz Festival, and Lincoln Center’s Bang on a Can Festival with Butch Morris' Chorus of Poets. Her upcoming young adult novel Marq and the Queen Fish is set for release in 2026.

  • Jay Rodriguez Sierra

    Creative Director

    Jay Rodriguez is a Grammy-nominated composer, producer, and internationally acclaimed multi-instrumentalist artist whose genre-blending sound has shaped music for over three decades. A creative force in New York City and beyond, Jay has collaborated with legends such as The Groove Collective, Elvis Costello, Ray Barretto, Prince, Celia Cruz, The Roots, and Fred Wesley. His solo album Your Sound, recorded live at Lincoln Center, showcases his signature blend of improvisation, soul, and global rhythms.
